Simulation Instructions

Welcome to Simul-AI-tor! This simulation is designed to help you practise clinical history taking and refine your clinical reasoning skills. Through this exercise, you'll engage with virtual patients while applying the Calgary–Cambridge model, enhanced with modern concepts of consent for physical examination and robust clinical reasoning strategies.

Initiating the Session

Overview:

Begin by warmly introducing yourself, confirming the patient's identity, and establishing a collaborative atmosphere. This sets the stage for an open dialogue.

Key Points:

Introduce yourself, obtain implicit consent to proceed with the consultation, and clearly outline the consultation process.

Gathering Information

Overview:

Use open-ended questions to allow the patient to share their symptoms, concerns, and relevant history. Active listening and reflective summarising are vital.

Key Points:

Encourage a detailed narrative, ask follow-up questions, and ensure that all critical information is captured.

Physical Examination & Consent

Overview:

Prior to any physical examination, obtain explicit consent by explaining its purpose and procedure. This demonstrates respect and maintains patient dignity.

Key Points:

Clearly request consent, explain each step of the examination, and integrate how the findings will contribute to your clinical reasoning.

Explanation & Planning

Overview:

Share your initial impressions and reasoning with the patient. Discuss potential diagnoses and outline the next steps, incorporating both hypothetico-deductive and Bayesian approaches.

Key Points:

Explain how you update your diagnostic probabilities with new information and how the plan will evolve based on both intuitive and analytical reasoning.

Closing the Session

Overview:

Summarise the consultation, review the key points, and confirm the patient’s understanding. Ensure that any uncertainties are addressed before ending the session.

Key Points:

Recap the main discussion, check for understanding, and clearly outline any follow-up or next steps.

Building a Relationship

Overview:

Develop a strong therapeutic alliance by demonstrating empathy, actively listening, and validating the patient’s experiences. This is foundational for effective care.

Key Points:

Show genuine concern, respect the patient’s perspective, and foster trust throughout the consultation.

Providing Structure

Overview:

Organise the consultation into clear, sequential phases. A structured approach ensures comprehensive history taking and supports effective clinical reasoning.

Key Points:

Follow a consistent framework and integrate clinical reasoning strategies—balancing intuitive judgements with analytical thought—to guide the consultation.
