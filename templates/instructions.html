Simulation Instructions

Welcome to Simul-AI-tor! This simulator is designed to help you practice the clinical history taking process. Through this exercise, you'll engage with virtual patients to better understand their complaint, consider potential causes and suggest a management plan. The app is not testing your diagnostic ability necessarily, but will give you feedback on your clinical assessment performance which will allow you to reflect and improve!

Initiating the Session

Overview:

Begin by warmly introducing yourself, confirming the patient's identity, and establishing a collaborative atmosphere. This sets the stage for an open dialogue.

Key Points:

Introduce yourself, show empathy and obtain consent to proceed with the consultation. You may wish to outline the process for the consultation.

Gathering Information

Overview:

Use a balance of open-ended and closed questions to allow the patient to share their symptoms, concerns, and relevant history. Active listening and reflective summarising are vital. Open questions tend to encourage the patients agenda whilst closed questions often lean in to your agenda - so keep this in mind.

Key Points:

Encourage a detailed narrative, ask follow-up questions, and ensure that all critical information is captured.

Physical Examination & Consent

Overview:

Prior to any physical examination, obtain explicit consent by explaining its purpose and procedure. This demonstrates respect and maintains patient dignity.

Key Points:

Clearly request consent, explain each step of the examination, and integrate how the findings will contribute to your clinical reasoning. Once consent is gained, click the button to retrieve live physical examination results. You should then discuss these with the patient.

Explanation & Planning

Overview:

Share your initial impressions and reasoning with the patient. Discuss potential differential diagnoses and their likelihood (Bayesian Reasoning).

Key Points:

Consider explaining how you update your diagnostic probabilities with new information and how the plan will evolve based on both intuitive and analytical reasoning.

Closing the Session

Overview:

Summarise the consultation, review the key points, and confirm the patient’s understanding. Ensure that any uncertainties are addressed before ending the session.

Key Points:

Recap the main discussion, check for understanding, and clearly outline any follow-up, safety-netting and/ or next steps.

Building a Relationship

Overview:

Develop a strong therapeutic alliance by demonstrating empathy, actively listening, and validating the patient’s experiences. This is foundational for effective care.

Key Points:

Show genuine concern, respect the patient’s perspective, and foster trust throughout the consultation.

Providing Structure

Overview:

Organise the consultation into clear, sequential phases. A structured approach ensures comprehensive history taking and supports effective clinical reasoning. One approach is history of presenting complaint, past medical history, medication history and allergies, relevant family history and finally social history

Key Points:

Follow a consistent framework and integrate clinical reasoning strategies—balancing intuitive judgements with analytical thought—to guide the consultation.
